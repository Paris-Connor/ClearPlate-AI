ClearPlate AI

Classifying Clear vs. Obscured License Plates Using AlexNet

Overview Technical Approach

Why License Plate Clarity Matters

Automatic License Plate Recognition (ALPR) systems are used for tolling, traffic monitoring, parking, and public safety. In practice, license plates are rarely “perfect”: they can be covered by dirt, glare, snow, motion blur, or even intentionally obscured. These real-world conditions make it hard for ALPR systems to read plates reliably.

ClearPlate AI explores whether a convolutional neural network (AlexNet) can learn to distinguish between clear plates and obscured plates. The goal is not to read the plate number itself, but to flag “hard” images that may need extra processing or human review.

Project Snapshot

  • Task: Binary image classification
  • Classes: Clear vs. Obscured license plates
  • Model: AlexNet (PyTorch), plus ResNet18 for comparison
  • Runs: 100+ training experiments (batch size, LR, augmentation, architecture)
  • Tools: Google Colab, PyTorch, Weights & Biases

Problem Statement

Many ALPR systems assume that the input image is “good enough” to read. In reality, a significant fraction of plates are partially blocked, reflective, or dirty. Instead of treating all images the same, it would be useful to have a model that first answers a simpler question:

“Is this license plate clear enough for standard processing, or is it obscured and likely to cause errors?”

A clarity classifier could be inserted at the front of an ALPR pipeline to:

  • Route “clean” images to the standard recognition system.
  • Send “obscured” images to enhanced processing (denoising, super-resolution) or human review.
  • Provide statistics on how often plates are unreadable in a given environment.

Project Goals

Core Objectives

  • Build a labeled dataset of clear vs. obscured license plate images.
  • Adapt AlexNet for binary classification on this dataset.
  • Compare pretrained, baseline, and untrained AlexNet variants, plus ResNet18.
  • Study batch size, learning rate, and data augmentation effects on accuracy.

Big Picture Motivation

This project connects deep learning to a concrete, messy real-world problem. Rather than training on a huge benchmark dataset, ClearPlate AI asks: What can we do with a small, hand-collected dataset that looks more like real traffic camera footage?

Even if the model is not perfect, learning how initialization, augmentation, and architecture choices affect performance is directly relevant to deploying trustworthy AI in safety-critical systems.
